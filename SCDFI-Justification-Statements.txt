SCDFI v0.4 — Clinical Justification Statements
================================================
Rachel Terra Norton, MS, CCC-SLP


FULL (Evaluation Reports)
-------------------------
The Social-Communication and Developmental Functioning Inventory (SCDFI v0.4) was used to assess the student's social communication and developmental functioning across 12 domains. The SCDFI was selected because it provides a comprehensive, strengths-based, multi-informant framework that integrates constructs from established models including the Learning Style Profile (LSP; Prizant & Wetherby), Functional Emotional Developmental Capacities (FEDC/DIR; Greenspan & Wieder), the Behavioral, Emotional, and Social Skills Inventory (BESSI; Soto & John), the Collaborative Problem Solving model and Assessment of Skills and Unsolved Problems (CPS/ASUP; Greene), the Social Skills Framework (SSF), Roth and Worthington's social communication hierarchy, and ASHA social communication benchmarks. This tool was developed to address the need for an integrated protocol that captures social communication functioning across clinical, educational, home, and community contexts through clinician, teacher, parent/caregiver, and self-report versions — allowing for cross-informant comparison and individualized, contextually sensitive goal development aligned with the unique transdisciplinary needs of our school community.


BRIEF (Progress Reports / Meeting Notes)
-----------------------------------------
The SCDFI v0.4 was administered to evaluate social communication and developmental functioning across 12 domains using clinician, teacher, and parent/caregiver report. The SCDFI integrates constructs from the LSP, FEDC/DIR, BESSI, CPS/ASUP (Greene), SSF, Roth & Worthington, and ASHA benchmarks to provide a strengths-based, multi-informant profile of the student's social communication skills across contexts.
